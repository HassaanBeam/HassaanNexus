# SWOT Analysis

**Purpose**: Assess Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats

## The Four Quadrants

|  | Helpful | Harmful |
|--|---------|---------|
| **Internal** | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| **External** | Opportunities | Threats |

## Questions to Ask

- **Strengths**: What advantages do we have? What do we do well? What resources do we have?
- **Weaknesses**: What could we improve? What do we lack? Where are we vulnerable?
- **Opportunities**: What trends could we exploit? What gaps exist in the market?
- **Threats**: What obstacles do we face? What is the competition doing? What changes threaten us?

## Strategic Combinations

- **S+O**: Use strengths to capture opportunities
- **W+O**: Address weaknesses to capture opportunities
- **S+T**: Use strengths to defend against threats
- **W+T**: Address weaknesses to avoid threats

## Process

1. Brainstorm items for each quadrant
2. Prioritize most important in each
3. Look for S+O combinations (offensive strategies)
4. Look for W+T combinations (defensive strategies)
5. Create action plan

## Tips

- Be honest about weaknesses
- Get multiple perspectives
- Focus on actionable insights
- Update periodically

## Output

2x2 SWOT matrix with strategic implications
